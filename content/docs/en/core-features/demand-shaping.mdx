# Demand Shaping

Demand Shaping lets you influence *when* and *how much* customers buy, rather than just reacting to what they do.

## Use cases

- **Shift demand** from peak periods to quieter times to smooth out warehouse load
- **Accelerate sell-through** on slow-moving inventory with targeted discounts
- **Bundle products** to increase average order value

## Creating a demand shaping campaign

1. Go to **Repricing → Demand Shaping** (or search for "demand shaping").
2. Click **New Campaign**.
3. Choose a campaign type:
   - **Flash sale** — temporary price reduction for a set window
   - **Bundle discount** — discount applies when two or more products are in the cart
   - **Tiered pricing** — lower price per unit at higher quantities
4. Set the start/end dates and the discount parameters.
5. Select which products or categories are in scope.
6. Click **Launch**.

## Monitoring campaign performance

During an active campaign, the **Campaign Dashboard** tab shows:

- Units sold per hour vs the pre-campaign baseline
- Revenue impact
- Estimated margin impact

## Ending a campaign early

Click **End Campaign** to revert prices immediately. The repricing rule (if any) that was active before the campaign will resume automatically.

## Best practices

- Run flash sales for 4–24 hours — longer campaigns lose urgency.
- Always check your min price guardrail before launching a discount.
- Use the Forecasting module to predict post-campaign demand dip (customers may have bought early).
