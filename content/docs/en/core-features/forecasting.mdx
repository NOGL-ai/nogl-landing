# Forecasting

The Forecasting module projects future demand and revenue using historical sales data, seasonal patterns, and market signals.

## Accessing forecasts

Navigate to **Dashboard → Forecasting** or use the global search (⌘K) and type "forecast".

## How forecasts work

NOGL uses a time-series model trained on:

- Your past 12 months of sales data
- Category-level seasonal indices
- Competitor price movements in your tracked companies

Forecasts are regenerated every 24 hours and cover a **90-day horizon** by default.

## Reading the forecast chart

The chart shows three bands:

| Band | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Blue line** | Expected (median) units sold per day |
| **Light blue shading** | 80% confidence interval |
| **Gray dashed line** | Last year's actuals (same period) |

## Adjusting the forecast

You can apply manual overrides when you have information the model doesn't (e.g. a planned promotion):

1. Click **Add override** above the chart.
2. Select the date range.
3. Enter the expected uplift or reduction (e.g. +30%).
4. Add a note explaining the override.

Overrides are highlighted in orange on the chart and are logged for audit purposes.

## Exporting forecast data

Click **Export → CSV** to download the full 90-day forecast with confidence intervals.

## Limitations

- Forecasts require at least 90 days of historical data.
- Products launched in the last 30 days use a **cold-start model** based on category averages.
- Forecasts do not account for events outside the data (e.g. supply chain disruptions unless manually overridden).
