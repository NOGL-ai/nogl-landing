# Repricing Rules

Auto-repricing rules watch your tracked competitors and adjust your prices automatically within guardrails you define.

## Creating a rule

1. Go to **Repricing → Auto Repricing Rules**.
2. Click **New Rule**.
3. Fill in:
   - **Name** — a label for internal reference
   - **Products** — choose individual SKUs or a category
   - **Strategy** — see strategies below
   - **Min price** and **Max price** — hard limits the rule cannot breach
4. Click **Save & Activate**.

## Strategies

| Strategy | Behavior |
|----------|---------|
| **Match cheapest** | Sets your price equal to the lowest tracked competitor |
| **Beat by amount** | Sets your price N euros below the cheapest competitor |
| **Beat by percent** | Sets your price N% below the cheapest competitor |
| **Stay within band** | Keeps your price within X% of the competitor average |
| **Maximize margin** | Raises your price as long as you remain the cheapest |

## Guardrails

Every rule requires a **minimum price** and **maximum price**. The rule will never set a price outside this range, regardless of competitor activity.

We recommend setting min price ≥ your cost + minimum acceptable margin.

## Rule priority

When a product matches multiple rules, the rule with the **highest priority number** wins. You can drag-and-drop rules in the list to reorder them.

## Viewing repricing history

Go to **Repricing → Auto Repricing History** to see a log of every price change made by a rule, including which competitor triggered it.

## Pausing rules

Click the toggle next to any rule to pause it. Paused rules do not apply changes but remain configured for easy reactivation.
