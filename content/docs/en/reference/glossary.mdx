# Glossary

**Average daily sales (ADS)**
The mean number of units sold per day, calculated over a rolling 14-day window.

**Beat by amount**
A repricing strategy that sets your price a fixed amount (e.g. €1.00) below the cheapest tracked competitor.

**Beat by percent**
A repricing strategy that sets your price a fixed percentage below the cheapest tracked competitor.

**Competitor match**
A link between one of your SKUs and a competitor's product page. Used to pull the competitor's price into repricing rules and alerts.

**Cold-start model**
The demand forecast model used for products with fewer than 90 days of sales history. Based on category-level averages.

**Days of stock**
Current inventory quantity divided by average daily sales. Indicates how many days until stockout at current sales velocity.

**Guardrail**
A hard minimum or maximum price that a repricing rule can never breach, regardless of competitor pricing.

**Lead time**
The number of days between placing a purchase order and receiving the goods. Used in replenishment calculations.

**Margin**
`(Price − Cost) ÷ Price × 100`. Expressed as a percentage.

**Match cheapest**
A repricing strategy that sets your price equal to the cheapest tracked competitor.

**Reorder point**
The stock level at which a replenishment recommendation is triggered. Calculated as: `Lead time (days) × ADS + Safety stock`.

**Safety stock**
A buffer of extra inventory held to protect against demand spikes or supplier delays. Default: 7 days of ADS.

**SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)**
A unique identifier for a product variant (e.g. `TSHIRT-L-BLK` for a large black t-shirt).

**Tracked competitor**
A competitor website or product listing that NOGL scrapes on a scheduled basis.

**Velocity**
Synonymous with average daily sales; how quickly a product sells.
